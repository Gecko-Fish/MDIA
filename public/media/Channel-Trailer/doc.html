Channel Trailer

Difficulty (★★★★☆)

Youtubers need to create a video trailer to introduce their audience to their channel. This is an extensive process that involves scripting, filming, and editing. Utilizing GenAI tools, one can speed up the time it takes to create a channel trailer and improve efficiency.

Tools

  1. Your own footage of yourself or HeyGen (paid)
  2. ChatGPT
  3. Runway
  4. HeyGen
  5. CapCut or Canva AI

Overview

  1. Brainstorming - ChatGPT
  1. The YouTuber should consider what makes their channel unique, the target audience, and the message they want to convey.
  1. Scriptwriting/Storyboarding - ChatGPT
  1.  Once the concept is clear, the YouTuber writes a script outlining the content of the trailer. This script should be concise, engaging, and highlight the key aspects of the channel.
  2. Some YouTubers may choose to create a storyboard to plan out the visual sequence of the trailer. This helps in visualizing how the video will flow and ensures that all necessary shots are included.
  1. Gathering Assets - Self
  1. The YouTuber collects all the necessary assets for the trailer, such as video clips, images, music, and graphics. They may use existing content from their channel or create new material specifically for the trailer.
  1. Recording of themselves- Self
  1. Record any on-camera segments or voiceovers.
  1. Digital Avatar Creation - HeyGen
  1. Use your self data to create your digital self avatar.
  2. Generating script for your avatar to speak and generating video for you avatar to perform.
  1. Digital Avatar Animation - Wonder Dynamics
  1. Use MoCap technology to replace a real figure in the video to an animated character.
  1. Any other video footage - Runway
  1. Record any other video footage as needed (e.g. b roll)
  2. Generate background video for your avatar.

  1. Editing - CapCut AI

  1. Using video editing software, the YouTuber edits the footage together according to the script and storyboard. This involves cutting and arranging clips, adding transitions, text overlays, music, and other visual effects to enhance the trailer.
  2. Export/Finalization

Text to Video Workflow Steps:

⚙️Step 1: In this workflow, we will be using Runway’s text-to-image generative AI tool. To access this tool, click on the “Start with Text” button. We start at the landing page of Runway after you have created an account and logged into it.

⚙️Step 3: Here you will see a text box. Where you see the yellow box with the placeholder “Use text to describe your scene and how it moves”, type in whatever prompt you want. A prompt is the video you would like to create and what you would like the video to contain.

For example, you can type “Cartoon girl looking out to the sunset in Kyoto”. You can also type  “Cartoon girl looking out to the sunset in Kyoto, gentle movement” to specify what kind of movement you would like the characters or the scene in your video to do.

After you have entered your prompt, click on the “Generate” button on the bottom right hand corner of the textbox. The “4s” represents the duration of your generated video (4s = 4 seconds).

⚙️Step 4: You did it! You have generated your very own 4 second video :)

⚙️Step 5: To save the video, click on the left most icon on the top right-hand corner of the video.

Editing

To edit the video together, we can use CapCut, which has AI tools integrated in it. For the purposes of this workflow, we’ll assume that you have a CapCut account and have already gone through the onboarding process.

Set Up

  • According to CapCut’s website, CapCut is accessible on any desktop device with Windows, Mac, Android, and iOS. CapCut is available 1) online or 2) for download for Windows and Mac. For this tutorial, the desktop version of CapCut will be used on Windows.

⚙️Step 1: Let’s get started! To create a new project, click on the “Create project” button.

⚙️Step 2: CapCut begins with a tutorial walkthrough that will prove to be extremely helpful. We’ll follow this format when we begin editing our video. Let’s click through the rest of the tutorial.

⚙️Step 3: We’ll see this screen once we’ve finished the tutorial. Let’s import our materials from our device, which are the Runway video that was generated and the video of ourselves. To do so, let’s click on the “Import” button.

⚙️Step 4: Click on the “+” button to add your videos to the timeline. You will see an “Added” marker on the top left corner of the video to signify that the video has been added to the timeline.

Use AI to auto-caption

⚙️Step 5: To add auto captions, you can click on the “Auto captions” button. CapCut AI will automatically generate captions for you.

⚙️Step 6: You can see the generated captions here. They should be synced to match the video.

⚙️Step 7: Under the “Basic” and “Templates” text, you can change the style of the captions if desired!

⚙️Step 8: To make necessary edits to the captions, you can either edit directly on the video player or in the black textbox.

Edit the video

⚙️Step 9: To edit the video to get rid of filler scenes or excess footage, you can drag the white slider in the editing timeline view to trim sections.

⚙️Step 10: To freeze a section of the video, hover your slider over the desired frame and click the  “Freeze” button. This will create a 3-second snapshot of the frame you select immediately after.

 

⚙️Step 11: To add text, click on the “Add text” button. Once it appears on your video, you can edit the text, drag it anywhere, rotate it, etc. Use CTR + C and CTR + V (and equivalent Macbook commands) to duplicate the text.

⚙️Step 12: You’ll see all the text show up in the editor window as well. Drag on the white sliders to position them in the video’s timeline.

⚙️Step 13: To add stickers to spruce up your videos, click on the stickers tab and either search or scroll through the selection.

⚙️Step 14: You can drag the white slider to position the stickers in the timeline, and drag your stickers around the screen to position it in the video / to scale!

⚙️Step 15: You can add effects for cool transitions - I used gradient wipe.

Add audio

⚙️Step 16: Let’s add an audio track by clicking on the “Audio” button and scrolling through. Click on the “+” button to add your track to the video.

⚙️Step 17: To easily split the track to fit your video, drag your slider to the end of the video and click on the “Split” button.

⚙️Step 18: To delete the excess track, click the “Delete” button.

⚙️Step 19: To adjust the sound of the audio, drag the Volume slider to the left to lower the sound and to the right to increase it..

⚙️Step 20: We’re ready to export! Click on the blue “Export” button.

⚙️Step 21: We’re almost there! Leave the default settings or modify them and click on the blue “Export” button.

You’re all done!!
