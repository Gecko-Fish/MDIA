Intro/Outro Content Creation Using Generative AI

Difficulty (★★☆☆☆)

YouTubers when creating their content tend to include some sort of an intro/outro scene that is embedded in the beginning and end of all their video content. It serves as a sort of “trademark”, or a representation that tells the watchers that they are currently watching the content of a specific Youtuber. To create an AI generated intro/outro video.

Tools

Troubleshooting

  • Runway AI has provided an extensive list of things to look out for when using their online platform. The list below is taken from their website as of [February 23, 2024], but for the most updated list of troubleshooting tips, please go to their official website. The link to their website is here.
  • Please ensure Runway AI is not experiencing a service outage - a period of time when the Runway application won’t be available/accessible to you. To know if Runway AI is experiencing a service outage, please go to their official status page and scroll down to the section named “App”. If everything on the website is currently “Operational”, this means the Runway application is NOT under maintenance. If the app is experiencing “Partial Outage” or “Major Outage”, this may mean the application is not available, and you should try accessing it another time.
  • To avoid running into technical issues, please ensure you are using Google Chrome, or any other Chromium browser. While Runway can open on other browsers such as Firefox and Safari, you may encounter technical issues down the line that can be avoided if you are using Chromium browsers.
  • If your Runway AI is not working properly, please try switching off any ad blocker, firewall, or VPN as these services may interrupt with Runway AI’s application. After turning off these services, please refresh your page before trying to use Runway AI again. If Runway AI works after you toggle these services off, this means these services are interrupting with your usage of the application. If Runway AI is still not working, something else is interrupting with your usage of Runway AI.
  • If Runway is still not working, you may need to whitelist Runway on your router/network. Whitelisting is the opposite of blacklisting, and it essentially means you have to explicitly allow Runway to run on your router/network. If you're using a company device or on a company network, your employer may also be running their own security features that are blocking Runway's tools from processing your inputs. To use Runway, you or your company's IT team will need to whitelist these essential Runway domains:
  • *.runwayml.com
  • *.runwayml.cloud
  • *.*.runwayml.cloud
  • You may need to clear your browser cache on Chrome. The steps you can follow to clear your cache can be accessed here.
  • You may need to disable any browser extensions. An extension is a small piece of software you can install to customise your browser’s appearance or function. To disable or uninstall your browser extensions, you can follow these steps.
  • You may also need to check your device’s and browser’s time and date settings and make sure they are identical AND correct. If both your device and your browser are not showing the identical/accurate time and date, your browser may fail to properly communicate with Runway's tools.
  • If the tool continues to not work, please contact Runway AI. You can submit a support request here.

Runway Gen-2 Video Prompting Tips

There are currently 3 modes to generate videos on Runway Gen-2 (the newest version of Runway):

  • Only Text to Video
  • Only Image to Video
  • Image + Text to Video

Below we will detail how to best prompt videos on Runway for Text to Video (These tips extend to Image + Text to Video). Currently Runway has no tips for Image to VideoThese steps are taken from the Runway Gen-2 Prompting Guide.

Prompting Styles

🌟What is a prompt? A prompt is what you insert into the AI generative tool (e.g. a body of text, an image, or both text and image, etc.) that instructs the AI to produce a desired outcome (e.g. a video in Runway).

Text to Video

  • This structure works well: [base prompt] in the style of [style], [aesthetic]
  • Insert your text in between the [ ]
  • To prompt Runway using text to video, you can type anything you want into the AI (for example, “Cartoon girl looking out to the sunset in Kyoto”) and Runway will create a video matching the body of text. Currently (as of February 28th 2024) there is no template you have to follow to generate a video using Runway.
  • To make your videos look better, you can use the following generic prompt modifiers (words that you can insert into your prompt that instructs the AI to enhance it). The following list is not exhaustive:
  • Masterpiece
  • Classic
  • Cinematic
  • For more action and movement (you should specify what kinds of movement you want a certain object in your video to do), you can try adding keywords like:
  • Cinematic action
  • First person view
  • Flying
  • Speeding
  • Running
  • You can also use camera-specific terms (these will affect how you view the scene you want to prompt). Imagine you are videoing the scene in front of you (the scene you are prompting), depending on where you stand, how much you zoom in, etc., you will be able to view your video from a different perspective. In general, trying to explain camera movements as if you were explaining to a toddler can help; so instead of dolly shot, you would say something like zooming in over time or growing over time:
  • Camera angles (full shot, close up, etc.)
  • Lens types (macro lens, wide angle, etc.)
  • Camera movements (slow pan, zoom, etc.)
  • You can explore Runway using Emojis too! Maybe it is hard for you to explain what you want the AI to do so using emojis can help you prompt more accurately 🐠🌸🌟

For extra prompting inspiration, make sure to check out Runway’s homepage (under Discover and Remix) where examples of prompts and what the prompts generate are displayed.

Text to Video Workflow Steps

⚙️Step 1: In this workflow, we will be using Runway’s text-to-video generative AI tool. To access this tool, click on the “Start with Text” button. We start at the landing page of Runway after you have created an account and logged into it.

⚙️Step 2: Here you will see a text box. Where you see the yellow box with the placeholder “Use text to describe your scene and how it moves”, type in whatever prompt you want. A prompt is the video you would like to create and what you would like the video to contain.

For example, you can type “Cartoon girl looking out to the sunset in Kyoto”. You can also type  “Cartoon girl looking out to the sunset in Kyoto, gentle movement” to specify what kind of movement you would like the characters or the scene in your video to do.

After you have entered your prompt, click on the “Generate” button on the bottom right hand corner of the textbox. The “4s” represents the duration of your generated video (4s = 4 seconds).

⚙️Step 3: You did it! You have generated your very own 4 second video :)

⚙️Step 4: To save the video, click on the left most icon on the top right-hand corner of the video.

Image to Video Workflow Steps

⚙️Step 1: In this workflow, we will be using Runway’s image-to-video generative AI tool. To access this tool, click on the “Start with Images” button. We start at the landing page of Runway after you have created an account and logged into it.

⚙️Step 2: Here you will see a box where you can “Drag and drop an image to be the start of the video”. You can also click on “upload a file” or “select from Assets” if you need to access your folders to acquire the image you want to use. For this tutorial, the “upload a file” option was selected.

⚙️Step 3: Navigate to the folder you have stored the image you would like to use to generate a video with.

⚙️Step 4: Drag and Drop (or double click) on the image you would like to use into the area highlighted by the yellow box.

⚙️Step 5 (OPTION A): Click on the “Generate” button on the bottom right hand corner of the textbox to generate a 4 second video using your selected image. To save the video, please follow Step 4 of the [Image to Video Workflow Steps]. You did it! You have generated your very own 4 second video :)

⚙️Step 5 (OPTION B): If you would like to add instructions that specifies what you would like Runway to generate, please navigate to the “IMAGE + DESCRIPTION” tab.

After typing in your prompt/description, click on the “Generate” button on the bottom right hand corner of the textbox to generate a 4 second video using your selected image. To save the video, please follow Step 4 of the [Image to Video Workflow Steps]. You did it! You have generated your very own 4 second video :)

Tips and Tricks!

To adjust the intensity of motion in your video, you can adjust the number using “General Intensity” highlighted above. The larger the number, the stronger the motion intensity is presented in your videos. For subtle and softer movements (which tend to look smoother), we suggest using a lower number (e.g. 2 or 3).

To extend the duration of the video, click on the “Extend 4s” button on the top. Then click on the “Extend 4s” button on the bottom right hand corner of the pop up titled “Extend Video”.

Video Editing Workflow Steps

⚙️Step 1: In this workflow, we will be using CapCut’s video editing tools. To access these tools, click on the “+ Create new” button. We start at the landing page of CapCut after you have created an account and logged into it.

⚙️Step 2: To edit the videos you have generated using Runway, please select the “16:9” option for optimal compatibility.

⚙️Step 3 (OPTION A): To retrieve the videos you have saved from Runway, please press one of the 3 icons within the yellow box, or the 2 icons in the right yellow box: The Computer icon (to access your computer files), the Drive icon (to access your Google Drive files), and the Box icon (to access your Dropbox files).

⚙️Step 3 (OPTION B): You can drag and drop a file from your desktop containing all the videos you wish to edit with into CapCut.

⚙️Step 3 (OPTION C): You can open a file on your desktop and drag and drop any videos into CapCut.

⚙️Step 4: After you have uploaded all the videos you want to edit, it will appear both on the left and at the bottom of your page.

Left (Repository): On the left you have a repository of all the videos you have uploaded onto CapCut. You can drag and drop the videos into the bottom box to combine and edit the videos.

Bottom (Edit Space): This is where you do all the editing for your videos.

⚙️Step 5: When you are satisfied with your video, to save your work please click on the “Export” video on the top right hand corner.

⚙️Step 6: You can either “Share on social” by selecting the platform icon you prefer or click on the “Download” button to save the video into your computer file.

⚙️Step 7: Please select the highest number under “Resolution”, select “High quality” under “Quality”, and select the highest fps under “Frame rate” for the highest quality video with a smooth finish. Finally, to save your video, please click the button “Export”. You are done!
